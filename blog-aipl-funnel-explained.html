What Is the AIPL Funnel?

Origin & Why It Fits E-Commerce

If you've ever wondered why your traffic doesn't translate to revenue, the answer is almost always hiding somewhere in your funnel. But not all funnel frameworks are created equal. The AIPL model — Awareness, Interest, Purchase, Loyalty — was originally developed within Alibaba's e-commerce ecosystem and has since been adapted for global B2B commerce.

What makes AIPL uniquely powerful is that it treats post-purchase loyalty as an equal stage, not an afterthought. In B2B, where customer lifetime value dwarfs first-order value, this distinction is critical.

AIPL vs. AIDA vs. Pirate Metrics (AARRR)

AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action) stops at the purchase. Pirate Metrics (AARRR) is designed for SaaS, not commerce. AIPL bridges both worlds — it's purpose-built for e-commerce businesses that need to track the full journey from first impression to repeat buyer. The Loyalty stage captures reorder rates, referral behavior, and account expansion — metrics that matter most in B2B.

Breaking Down Each Stage

Awareness — Are You Visible Where Buyers Search?

The first stage measures whether your brand appears where B2B buyers are actively looking. This includes paid search, LinkedIn, industry publications, trade directories, and increasingly, AI search engines. Key metrics: impressions, reach quality, brand search volume, and share of voice in your category.

Interest — Are Visitors Engaging or Bouncing?

Once aware, prospects need a reason to stay. This stage covers landing page engagement, product catalog browsing, content consumption, and initial inquiries. The critical signal here is the gap between your acquisition messaging and on-site experience. If your ads promise one thing but your landing page delivers another, interest dies immediately.

The gap between awareness and interest is where most B2B marketing budgets go to die.

Purchase — Is Your Checkout Converting or Leaking?

The conversion stage. For B2B, this is more complex than B2C — involving quotes, approval workflows, procurement processes, and sometimes multi-stakeholder sign-offs. Optimizing this stage means reducing friction at every decision point: cart to checkout, payment selection, order confirmation. Key metrics: cart-to-purchase rate, checkout abandonment rate, and average order value.

Loyalty — Are Buyers Coming Back?

The most undervalued and highest-leverage stage. Loyalty encompasses reorder rates, account expansion, referrals, NPS scores, and customer lifetime value. In B2B, acquiring a new customer costs 5-7x more than retaining an existing one. Brands that optimize for loyalty see compounding returns that dwarf acquisition-only strategies.
